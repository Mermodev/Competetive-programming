## What is this repository for?

This repository contains my solutions to problems from various competitive programming platforms such as [CSES](https://cses.fi/), [SIO2](https://sio2.mimuw.edu.pl/c/oi33-1/dashboard/), and [Codeforces](https://codeforces.com/).

Some tasks may no longer be publicly available. In such cases, I try to include the original statement and a checker or stress-testing program so that others can still attempt the problem themselves.

Besides problem solutions, the repository also contains various tools and utilities I use for competitive programming, including:
- C++ headers and templates
- compilation aliases
- `.vimrc` and `.bashrc` configuration
- stress testers and generators
- debugging setups
- snippets and helper scripts

The repository mainly serves as both:
1. an archive of my solutions and progress, and  
2. a collection of utilities and setups that may be useful for other competitive programmers.

Disclaimer: I am an amateur competitive programmer, so some solutions or tools may be unconventional, overengineered, or simply not optimal.

---

## Are the submitted solutions correct?

Unless explicitly marked otherwise (for example by using a different filename format than `task_name.cpp`, or by a note at the top of the file), all submitted solutions passed the official tests on the platform they were submitted to.

However, accepted does not necessarily mean optimal. Many solutions were written under contest conditions, so while they are correct and efficient enough to pass, they may not always be the cleanest, fastest, or most elegant implementations possible.

There may also still be:
- unnecessary complexity,
- questionable coding style,
- leftover debug code,
- or missed edge cases not covered by official tests.

If you find any mistakes or have suggestions for improvements, feel free to open an issue or pull request.
